How to Spot Phishing Emails

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Suspicious sender addresses: Check for misspellings or suspicious domains
  • Urgent language: "Act now!" "Your account will be closed!" "Immediate action required!"
  • Generic greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
  • Suspicious links: Hover over links to see where they really go
  • Unexpected attachments: Be cautious of unexpected files
  • Grammar and spelling errors: Professional companies proofread their emails
  • Requests for personal information: Legitimate companies won't ask for passwords via email

What to look for in legitimate emails:

  • Proper company branding and professional formatting
  • Your actual name and account details
  • Clear, professional language
  • Valid company contact information
  • Consistent domain names

Best Practices:

  • When in doubt, contact the company directly through their official website
  • Never click suspicious links - type the website URL manually
  • Keep your software and antivirus updated
  • Use two-factor authentication when available
